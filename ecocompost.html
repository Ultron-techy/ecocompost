Seed Bombs

Seed balls, sometimes called seed bombs, are a fascinating and eco-friendly way to plant seeds. Here's how they work:

What they are:Seed balls are small, round clumps made of soil, clay, and seeds. They can be thrown or placed in areas where you want plants to grow.
How they work:The clay protects the seeds from being eaten by birds or washed away by rain. When it rains, the clay dissolves, allowing the seeds to sprout.


Why They Are Useful:
Easy to use: Just throw them in the desired location.
Versatile: You can use them in gardens, vacant lots, or even hard-to-reach places.
Eco-friendly: Promotes the growth of plants and flowers, supporting local ecosystems.

making of seed balls
Gather Materials: Seeds, clay powder, compost or potting soil, and water.
Mix Ingredients: Combine the seeds with the clay and compost.
Shape into Balls: Add water and form the mixture into small balls.
Dry: Let them dry out completely before using.

here's a simple explanation of the structure of a seed ball: Outer Clay Layer: This forms the protective shell, which keeps the seeds safe from birds, pests, and harsh weather conditions.
Soil/Compost Layer: Just inside the clay, there's a mix of nutrient-rich soil or compost that provides a nurturing environment for the seeds to germinate.
Seeds: At the core of the ball, the seeds are nestled within the soil or compost.
